PS History

SN Name Year Description
1 Playstation One 1997 Released on July 7, 2000,concurrently with its successor the PlayStation 2, the PS One (stylized as PS one) was a considerably smaller, redesigned version of the original PlayStation video game console.
2 Playstation 2 2001 Released in 2000, 15 months after the Dreamcast and a year before its other competitors, the Xbox and the Nintendo GameCube, the PlayStation 2 is backwards-compatible with most original PlayStation games.
3 Playstation 3 2006 Released on November 11, 2006 7in Japan, the PlayStation 3 (PS3) is a seventh generation game console from Sony.The PS3 is the first console in the series to introduce the use of motion-sensing technology and features high-definition resolution.
4 Playstation 4 2013 PlayStation 4 (PS4) was announced by Sony Computer Entertainment at a press conference on February 20, 2013.According to lead system architect, Mark Cerny, development on the PlayStation 4 began as early as 2008.
5 Playstation 5 2021 The PlayStation 5 (PS5)was released worldwide on November 12, 2020, and, alongside the Xbox Series X and Series S released the same month, is part of the ninth generation of video game consoles.

Microsoft Xbox History

SN Name Year Description
1 Xbox 2001 The Xbox was released on November 15, 2001 .As part of the sixth generation of video game consoles, the Xbox competed with Sony's PlayStation 2, Sega's Dreamcast
2 Xbox 360 2005 The Xbox 360 was released as the successor of the original Xbox in November 2005, competing with Sony's PlayStation 3 and Nintendo's Wii as part of the seventh generation of video game consoles. As of June 2014, 84 million Xbox 360 consoles have been sold worldwide.
3 Xbox One 2013 The Xbox One was released on November 22, 2013, in North America, as the successor to the Xbox 360. The Xbox One competes with Sony's PlayStation 4 and Nintendo's Wii U and Switch as part of the eighth generation of video game consoles.
4 Xbox Series X and Series S 2020 The fourth generation of Xbox models, simply named Xbox,[43] includes the Xbox Series X and Xbox Series S that launched on November 10, 2020. Both are considered members of the ninth generation of video game consoles alongside the PlayStation 5, also released that month.
